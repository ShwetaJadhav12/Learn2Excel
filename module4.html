üè° Gardening Techniques

1. Direct Sowing

Seeds are sown directly into the soil where they will grow permanently.

2. Transplanting

Seeds are first grown in seed trays or pots and then transferred to the garden once they develop a strong root system.

3. Companion Planting

Growing certain plants together to improve growth and repel pests.

4. Square Foot Gardening

A high-density planting technique where crops are grown in square-foot sections.

5. Mulching

Covering the soil surface with organic material like straw, grass clippings, or wood chips.

6. Vertical Gardening

Growing plants upward using trellises, walls, or vertical planters.

üåæ Farming Techniques

1. Conventional Tilling

Involves plowing the land to prepare the soil for planting.

2. No-Till Farming

Seeds are planted directly into the soil without disturbing it.

3. Crop Rotation

Growing different crops in a specific sequence to maintain soil health.

4. Intercropping

Growing two or more crops together in the same field.

5. Terrace Farming

Practiced on hilly areas where land is cut into steps (terraces) to reduce erosion and water runoff.

6. Drip Irrigation

A controlled watering method where water drips slowly to the roots of plants through tubes or emitters.

7. Hydroponics

Growing plants without soil, using a nutrient-rich water solution.

8. Greenhouse Farming

Growing plants in a controlled environment to protect them from extreme weather.
