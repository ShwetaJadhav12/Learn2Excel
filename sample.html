Introduction to Gardening and Farming

What is Gardening?

Gardening is the practice of cultivating plants in small areas like backyards, balconies, or terraces. It involves growing flowers, vegetables, and herbs for personal use.

Types of Gardening:

  • Container Gardening
  • Vertical Gardening
  • Herb Gardening
  • Flower Gardening
  • Indoor Gardening

What is Farming?

Farming involves large-scale production of crops and livestock for commercial or subsistence purposes. It requires more land and specialized knowledge compared to gardening.

Types of Farming:

  • Crop Farming
  • Livestock Farming
  • Organic Farming
  • Mixed Farming
  • Agroforestry

Difference Between Gardening and Farming

Aspect Gardening Farming
Scale Small-scale Large-scale
Purpose Personal use, hobby Commercial production
Tools Hand tools Machinery
Watering Manual or small-scale irrigation Irrigation systems

Common Elements

  • Soil health
  • Watering and sunlight
  • Pest control
  • Plant nutrition
  • Harvesting

Benefits of Gardening and Farming

  • Health and Wellbeing: Reduces stress and provides fresh food.
  • Environmental Benefits: Improves air quality and reduces soil erosion.
  • Economic Benefits: Lowers grocery costs and supports local markets.
