Introduction to Farming

Farming is one of the oldest human activities, dating back thousands of years. 🌾 It provides us with food, clothing, and even fuel! From growing crops to raising livestock, farming plays a crucial role in sustaining civilizations.

Whether it’s a high-tech commercial farm or a humble backyard garden, **farming is basically nature’s version of a startup—except the investors are the sun, rain, and soil.** ☀️🌧️🌱

Types of Farming

1. Subsistence Farming

Farming mainly for personal survival. Families grow just enough food for themselves, and maybe the neighbors if they’re feeling generous. 🍚

2. Commercial Farming

Farming as a business. Large-scale production of crops and livestock for sale in markets. Think of it as **"farming meets capitalism."** 💰

3. Organic Farming

No synthetic fertilizers or pesticides, just natural goodness! 🌱 The kind of farming that makes you feel extra healthy just by looking at it.

4. Intensive Farming

High-input farming that maximizes yield per unit of land. Think of it as **"doing the most with the least."** 🚜

5. Extensive Farming

Large farms with minimal labor. If land had a **"low-maintenance mode,"** this would be it. 🏞️

6. Plantation Farming

Large-scale farming of cash crops like tea, coffee, and rubber. Basically, where your morning coffee starts. ☕

Difference Between Gardening and Farming

Aspect Gardening Farming
Scale Small-scale Large-scale
Purpose Personal use, hobby Commercial production
Tools Hand tools Machinery
Watering Manual or small-scale irrigation Irrigation systems
