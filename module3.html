Introduction

Farming and gardening in India are deeply influenced by diverse climates, soil types, and traditional practices. This guide will help you understand the best seed selection, suitable soil types, and composting methods to enhance productivity.

Seed Selection & Preparation

Choosing the right seeds depends on soil type and climate.

  • For Summers: Opt for heat-resistant seeds like millets and okra.
  • For Winters: Prefer wheat, carrots, and peas.
  • Hybrid vs. Indigenous: Hybrid seeds yield more but need more care, while indigenous seeds are more resilient.
Soil Types & Suitability

Different soil types suit different crops.

  • Alluvial Soil: Best for wheat, rice, and sugarcane.
  • Black Soil: Ideal for cotton and soybean.
  • Red Soil: Suitable for groundnuts and pulses.
  • Laterite Soil: Used for tea, coffee, and cashews.
Composting Methods

Composting improves soil fertility naturally.

  • Vermicomposting: Uses earthworms to break down organic matter.
  • Traditional Composting: Decomposes kitchen and farm waste over time.
  • Green Manure: Plant-based composting improves nitrogen levels.
  • Bokashi Composting: Uses microbes for faster breakdown.
